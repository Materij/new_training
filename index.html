Quote from St. Isaac's Cathedral

Here February 19 (March 1) married in 1712 Petr I and Ekaterina Alekseevna[3]. There is an entry in the log book for that day:

In the coming year, which no longer represented the expectation of adversity, Peter I got married to Ekaterina Alekseevna on the 19th, on Tuesday, during the omnivorous week. The wedding of His Majesty was made in the morning in St. Isaac's Cathedral. At 10 o'clock in the morning, the highly married, with volleys from the bastions of the Peter and Paul and Admiralty fortresses, entered their winter home[4].

Quote by Mayakovsky

                My verse
                        with labor will
                              break through the bulk of years
                and will appear
                           weighty,
                               rough,
                                     visibly,
                how the plumbing
                              came in today,
                made by the slaves
                                    of Rome.
        
— In a loud voice

Quote on Information Display

Ways to actively display information

The information presented on the web can be accessed:

  • only for reading («passively»);
  • for reading and adding/modifying («actively»).

Ways to actively display information on the World Wide Web include:

  • guest books (eng. guestbook);
  • форумы (eng. forum);
  • chats (eng. chat);
  • blogs (eng. blog);
  • wiki-projects;
  • социальные сети (eng. social networking service);
  • content management systems (eng. content management system, CMS).

This division is very conditional. So, say, a blog or a guest book can be considered as a special case of a forum, which, in turn, is a special case of a content management system. Usually the difference is manifested in the purpose, approach and positioning of a particular product.

Some information from websites can also be accessed through speech. In India started[3] testing a system that makes the text content of pages accessible even to humans, unable to read and write.
